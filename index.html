Patryk Radyjowski

Mechanical Engineering Ph.D. graduate student
The University of Texas at Austin

Born and raised in Opole, Poland. Left home in 2008 to chase my dreams. Many years and a multitude of unique experiences later I know it was the right decision.

Over the last 13 years, I have worked on both hardware and software projects. Furthermore, I gained hands-on experience with systems operating on both ends of the energetic spectrum: from cryogenic and vacuum to ultra-high temperatures and pressures.

I like to tinker and play with mechanical devices and electronics. On this webpage, I'd like to share some of the interesting projects I was part of.

Upon graduation (May 2021) I hope to find a job in the field of thermal design or additive manufacturing.
I embrace challenge and remain open-minded to any opening that would make a good use of my skillset.

Ph.D. Research

My graduate research is an interdisciplinary combination of combustion science and additive manufacturing.
I'm working on using a laser sintering to "3D print" ceramic-metal complex shape burners.
Thanks to a special design the released heat is recirculated back into the flame improving efficiency and enabling new modes of operation.

It has been a long journey that exposed me to many side topics such as:

  • biomass combustion - developing improved woodchip burning stoves
  • powder bed methods of additive manufacturing
  • high-temperature vacuum furnace operation and maintenance
  • many years of being a Teaching Assistant for introductory Thermodynamic class
  • managing a combustion laboratory and leading a team of undergraduate research assistants

  • Ellzey Combustion Group

M.Eng. Project

My Master's project was a design of cryogenic holder and cooling system for a superconductive coil.
The analytical and FEM modeling provided sufficient confidence to let me stay after my graduation to bring my desing to life.
My work was published in Superconductor Science and Technology journal in 2016.

Internship

Mitsubishi Electric Research laboratory

Project: 3D printer for extreme environments

Created a prototype of a UV-curable resin 3D printer to be used in vacuum.
I was responsible for a complete development cycle from CAD design, through various methods of manufacturing and finishing on an electronic control system.
This assignment heavily used my skills in experiment design, CAD (SolidWorks), machine shop operations, vacuum systems, and electronics (Arduino) My work was presented at International Astronautical Congress (IAC) 2019. Free-access link provided by MERL.
