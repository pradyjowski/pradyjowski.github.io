Ph.D. Research

My graduate research is interdisciplinary combination of combustion science and additive manufacturing. I'm working on using a laser sintering to "3D print" ceramic-metal complex shape burners. Thanks to a special design the released heat is recirculated back into the flame allowing for new modes of operation.

It has been a long journey that exposed me to many side topics such as:

  • biomass combustion - developing improved woodchip burning stoves
  • many year of being a Teaching Assistant for introductory Thermodynamic class
  • managing a combustion laboratory and leading a team of undergraduate research assistants

Reasearch Group: Ellzey Combustion Group

Previous to Ph.D. I have done my Master's project on design of cryogenic holder and cooling system for a superconductive coil. The analytical and FEM modeling provided sufficient confidence to let me stay after my graduation to bring my desing to life. For the picture of final result please check the gallery.

Internship at Mitsubishi Electric Research laboratory

Project: 3D printer for extreme environments

As an intern in the research laboratory I worked on creating a prototype of UV-curable resin 3D printer. I was responsible for a complete development cycle from CAD design, through varius methods of manufacturing and finishing on an electronic control system. This assignment heavily used my skills of experimental design, CAD (SolidWorks), machine shop operations, and consumer electronics (Arduino)

For more information see official publication: MERL Website
